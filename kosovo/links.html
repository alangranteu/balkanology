Kosovo :: Links

The sites listed on this page are specifically about Kosovo. Many of the more general sites listed in the Overview of the Balkans are also useful for planning a trip to Kosovo. I am aware that some of these links are now out of date while other useful sites have appeared - I plan to overhaul this page at some point in 2015.

Tourism and Transport

  • Kosova - Discover new beauty

    The official website for tourism in Kosovo. The information provided is rather limited, but it's a step in the right direction.

  • In Your Pocket - Kosovo

    Presumably bored with covering cliched tourist hotspots like Minsk and Tirana, the IYP crew have created the first English-language guide (probably) to Pristina, with lots of practical information that until now has been impossible to find online. They also have mini-guides to Peja and Prizren.

  • Discover Kosovo

    Detailed hotel listings covering both Pristina and other towns in Kosovo, along with some information about things to see and do.

  • Wikitravel - Kosovo

    Currently has quite a lot of detail about Pristina but little about other places in Kosovo.

  • A walk around Prizren

    An online extract from the Bradt Guide to Kosovo (see the Books page).

  • Kosovo Railways

    Few foreigners travel on domestic trains in Kosovo, but this site may be of interest for its information about the international train from Pristina to Skopje (Shkup).

  • Pristina Airport

    Details of services from Pristina - apparently honoured as the best airport in Europe in 2006.

  • Hotels Kosova

    Descriptions, reviews, and contact details of about a dozen hotels, mostly in Pristina. No online booking yet.

  • Rugova Experience

    This NGO organises outdoor activities in the mountains near Peja in western Kosovo.

Background

  • UNMIK Online

    Recent news and developments in Kosovo, as seen by the UN adminstration.

  • The cultural heritage of Prizren

    A photographic tour of Prizren's rich architectural and urban heritage. The photos were taken in 2000; some of the buildings features suffered further damage in 2004.
